Canadian Mental Health Resources

Calgary, A.B.

Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD)

CanLearn Society

Suite 100, 1117 Macleod Trail SE, Calgary, AB T2G 2M8

403-686-9300

https://www.canlearnsociety.ca/resources/adhd-ld-resources/adhd-families/

CanLearn Society helps individuals and families living with attention deficits to learn and thrive. CanLearn provides assessments and resources such as workshops focused on ADHD to help build awareness on the topic.

There are specific fees for different programs, however, an individual is able to apply for government assistance.

No medical referral is needed.

Alberta Counselling Center

280 Midpark Way SE, #210, Calgary, AB, T2X 1J6

(587) 352-3222

https://albertacounselling.ca/

Alberta Counselling Center provides psychoeducational assessments and counselling for those struggling from ADHD and other mental health difficulties.

Counselling fee: $200/50 min
Assessment fee: $1600-$2400

No medical referral is needed however your insurance company may ask for a referral.

Anxiety and Stress Management

Tammy McCroary Hypnotherapy

Calgary, AB

587-977-3342

http://tammymccroary.ca/anxiety-relief/

Tammy offers relief for individuals suffering from anxiety. She uses hypnotherapy and counselling methods focused on improving symptoms and teaching coping mechanisms.

Fees are determined on an individual basis. Tammy will have a consult with the client to see how many sessions, how intensive the sessions are, and what techniques will be used which all play a factor in the fee.

No medical referral is needed.

Serenity Now Wellness Center

1934 9 Ave SE, Calgary, AB, T2G 0V2

(403) 454-7600

http://serenitynowwellness.ca/

Serenity Now focuses on overcoming anxiety and stress through the use of mindfulness meditation, massage therapy, and holistic counselling services. There are packages that you can make to ensure you are getting the best treatment all in one place.

Counselling: $150-$200 per hour
Sliding scale options available

No medical referral is needed.

Bullying, Self-Harm and Suicide Prevention

Centre for Suicide Awareness

Suite 320, 105 12 Avenue S.E., Calgary, AB T2G 1A1

403-245-3900

https://www.suicideinfo.ca/

The Centre for Suicide Prevention is an education centre that offers workshops and intervention/prevention strategies for suicide.
They also have an In Crisis tab on their website that will bring you to all of the resources within your area.

Workshops: $100 to $250
Other resources: free

No medical referral is needed.

Distress Centre

999 8 St SW, Calgary, AB T2R 1J5

403-266-1601

https://www.distresscentre.com/

Distress Centre Calgary provides 24-hour crisis support in Calgary and provides counselling for issues that can't be resolved over the phone.

Services are free.

No medical referral is needed.

Calgary Counselling Centre

Suite 1000, 105 – 12 Avenue SE, Calgary, AB T2G 1A1, Canada

(403) 265-4980

https://calgarycounselling.com/

Counsels youth to help them build stronger and healthier relationships when facing violence, abuse, or bullying. The centre also offers numerous resources for adult and family counselling.

Services are sliding scale.

Mood Disorders (Depression, Postpartum Depression, Bipolar)

Hopeful Horizons

420, 1716 16th Ave NW, Calgary, Alberta, T2M 0L7

(403) 988-0944

http://hopefulhorizons.ca

Ashley, owner and registered psychologist, assists women in bettering their mental health. She has extensive experience with postpartum depression and anxiety. She uses numerous counselling methods to ensure the mother is able to return to her usual self without feelings of sadness, anger, or anxiety.

A 50 to 60-minute session is $180 but she does have limited sliding scale spots.

No medical referral is needed.

OBAD

1721 29 Avenue SW, Suite 375, Calgary, AB, T2T 6T7 Canada

Text or call: (403) 263-7408
Toll free: 1 (866) 263-7408

https://www.obad.ca

The Organization for Bipolar Affective Disorder (OBAD) is a non-for-profit organization that utilizes seven facilitators and operates six different drop-in peer support groups in Calgary and Edmonton. OBAD also offers drop-in sessions for families and individuals living with bipolar disorder. They also organize educational events, foster collaborations, creative experiences and workshops.

Services are free. The organization is funded by the government and grants.

No medical referral is needed.

Families Matter

#3404 25th street NE, Calgary, T1Y 6C1
403-205-5178 or

#101 239 Midpark Way SE, Calgary, T2X 1M2
403-288-1446

https://www.familiesmatter.ca

Families Matter’s mission is to help families thrive. They have a large postnatal mental health programming. They offer one-on-one support at home or in facility, support groups (prenatal and postpartum), and a program called “Baby Drop in” where mothers are able to bring their baby to have coffee and snacks with other moms and their baby.

Our public offerings are covered by generous funding from the Alberta Government as part of the Family Resource Network.

No medical referral is needed.

Substance Abuse and Addiction

Aventa

610-25 Avenue S.W., Calgary, Alberta, T2S 0L6, Canada

(403) 245-9050 Monday – Friday 8:00 am – 4:00 pm
(403) 541-4658 Evenings and Weekends

https://aventa.org/

Aventa offers a holistic treatment approach in helping women to overcome their addictions, and lead lives that are addiction free. They do this through teaching women to build resilience so that they can overcome issues with addiction in the future. The programming includes two live in facility phases.

The majority of funding is provided through the Ministry of Health and Alberta Health Services, Alberta Human Services and Calgary Child and Family Services. If there are additional fees required, they will be determined at the assessment appointment.

You need a referral from an AHS mental health counsellor.

Alberta Adolescent Recovery Centre (AARC)

303 Forge Road S.E., Calgary, Alberta, T2H 0S9

(403) 253-5250

https://www.aarc.ab.ca/

The AARC treatment program is specially designed for adolescents 12 to 21 years of age who are diagnosed with substance use disorder (DSM-5). AARC recognizes addiction as a complex interaction between the biological, psychological, social, and spiritual. They use peer counsellors, Family involvement, and Recovery Homes.

The majority of AARC clients receive a subsidy provided by donations and fundraising. Eligibility for help is based on need. The family’s daily fee rate will be determined by the length of time the adolescent will be in treatment.

No medical referral is needed.

Sunrise Healing Lodge

1231 34 Ave NE, Calgary, AB T2E 6N4

403-261-7921

http://nass.ca/

Providing addictions treatment through Indigenous Culture & the 12-step program of recovery. The program blends culture and the 12-step program, allowing our clients to work through all 12 steps while in treatment, using cultural practices to connect with their spirituality.

Fees vary depending on the program of choice.

No medical referral is needed.

Dementia and Alzheimer's

Carewest

6909 – 14th Street S.W. Calgary, Alberta, T2V 1P8

403-640-6480

http://carewest.ca/

The focus is on a live-in dementia program which assess clients, provide education, support clients, provide information, and link caregivers with ongoing support.

Average monthly fee is approx. 2,000 (This is regulated by the Alberta government).

You need a referral from a medical professional.

1100 8 Ave SW Unit #201
