Bullying, Self-Harm and Suicide Prevention

ERASE (Expect Respect And a Safe Education) Bullying

Victoria, BC

Focus is on creating a school environment where students are free from discrimination, bullying, harassment, intimidation, and violence; where clear expectations of acceptable behaviour are held for all members of the school community; and where there is a sense of connectedness. Helps to ensure that every child feels safe, accepted, and respected, regardless of gender, race, culture, religion, or sexual orientation. Builds on effective programs already in place and ensures there are consistent policies and practices backed by strong community partnerships and support. The Report It Tool allows any student in BC’s public schools to fill out a form and report cases of bullying anonymously if desired, whether the student was a victim or a witness. The tool sends a message to the student ‘s school or school district ‘s safe school coordinator, who will follow-up on the situation right away. Students may also report other issues of concern at school through the report form.

No Fee.

No Referral Required.

NEED2 - Youthspace.ca

Victoria, BC

778-783-0177 or support@youthspace.ca

www.youthspace.ca

Online support network for Canadian youth up to 30 years of age to access emotional support, resources, and crisis response services. Professionally trained volunteers risk assess, create safety plans, and link youth to other resources; available from 6 pm to 12 am PST, seven days a week through online chat or text messaging. Youth Talk Email Counselling offers support through email discussion with professional youth counsellors, in partnership with Pacific Centre Family Services Association. Also offers a comprehensive online resource database of mental health and wellness programs, with a focus on serving youth in the Greater Victoria area.

No Fee.

No Referral Required.

NEED2 Suicide Prevention, Education and Support

300-818 Douglas Street, Victoria, BC, V8W 2B6

250-386-6328 or admin@need2.ca

www.need2.ca

Works to prevent suicide and reduce stigma through community programs, training workshops, and accessible emotional and crisis support. Suicide Education and Awareness (SEA) Program provides free suicide prevention education to students in grades 8 and 10, and staff, throughout the Greater Victoria school system. Students learn about the warning signs of suicide, resources available to them, and how to help themselves and others. Support for Suicide Loss is a peer support group for adults who have lost a loved one to suicide; meets from 6:30 pm to 8:30 pm every first Thursday of the month. Non-profit society.

No Fee.

No Referral Required.

Substance Abuse and Addiction

Island Health

Rapid Access Addiction Clinic (RAAC) - Victoria

1119 Pembroke Street, Victoria, BC, V8T 1J3

250-213-4444 Local 0 / 250-519-3485

www.islandhealth.ca

Provides short-term support to individuals with problematic opioid use by starting and maintaining clients on opioid agonist (replacement) therapy such as Buprenorphine/Naloxone (Suboxone) or Methadone. Also provides harm reduction strategies, and referral to social and peer support services. Clients are enrolled in the multidisciplinary, intensive service while they stabilize and are supported as they transition to longer term community services. Offers free Take Home Naloxone Kits (Narcan) that can be used to reverse the effects of overdoses from opioids like fentanyl, heroin, methadone, morphine, and oxycodone. As per COVID-19 protocols, currently an access point for eligible individuals to secure safer drugs (opiates or alcohol only), as a response to the concurrent COVID-19 and opioid overdoes public health crises. Eligibility requirements include being at risk, suspected, or confirmed COVID-19 positive; having a history of ongoing active substance use; and at high risk of withdrawal, overdose, craving, or other harms related to drug use.

No Fee.

No Referral Required.

Victoria Native Friendship Centre

Addiction Services Program

231 Regina Avenue, Victoria, BC, V8Z 1J6

250-384-3211

www.vnfc.

Provides services to Indigenous individuals with substance abuse issues, in either harm reduction or abstinence/recovery approaches. Services include individual counselling, 12-step support groups, and referral to community services. Approach is holistic, and recognizes the significance of spiritual, cultural, psychological, and physical needs in developing a healthier lifestyle.
