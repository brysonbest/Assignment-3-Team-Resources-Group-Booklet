Adult ADHD Victoria BC

hello@adultadhdvictoriabc.com

Virtual Support Group Meetings

A peer-to-peer support group for adults with ADHD in the Victoria area.

No Fee.

No Referral Required.

BC Children's Hospital (BCCH)

Mental Health Building 85, 4500 Oak Street, Vancouver BC V6H 3N1

604-875-2010 / 1-888-300-3088

www.bcchildrens.ca

Provincial Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) Outpatient Program

Assesses and treats children and youth ages six to 18 with complex symptoms suggestive of attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD). Serves all of BC.

No Fee.

Medical referral required.

The Learning Curve

Unit 5-774 Bay Street, Victoria, BC V8T 5E4

250-370-9513 or info@ldasvi.bc.ca

https://ldasvi.bc.ca/

Provides workshops to children and their families. Aims to support children with Learning Disabilities and Attention Deficit Disorder. Offers individualized programming, as well as group programs focusing on emotional and academic skill development.

$20-$35+ per hour. Fees dependent on support type and number of sessions.

No Referral Required.

Anxiety and Stress Management

Canadian Mental Health Association (CMHA) - Victoria Branch

101-612 Fort Street, Victoria, BC, V8W 1J5

250-216-4228 or victoria.branch@cmha.bc.ca

www.victoria.cmha.bc.ca

Living Life to the Full & Bounce Back

CMHA supports two programs intended to address symptoms of anxiety and depression. These are available for free to all residents of BC. Programs support youth, adults, and seniors towards developing skills in managing depression, anxiety, and stress.

No Fee for Residents of BC.

No Referral Required.

Saanich Neighbourhood Place

3100 Tillicum Road, Victoria, BC, V9A 6T2

250-360-1148 or admin@snplace.org

www.saanichneighbourhoodplace.com

For children and youth. Also supplies parenting and family supports. Provides programming and information for dealing with anxiety and depression. Services include early intervention and therapeutic management delivered through both one-to-one supports and group settings. Will also assist in providing practical supports including food, job search aid, and extra-curricular activities.

No Fee.

No Referral Required.

YMCA-YWCA of Vancouver Island

851 Broughton Street, Victoria, BC, V8W 1E5

604-673-6182 or ymind@gv.ymca.ca

https://www.gv.ymca.ca/mental-wellness

Y Mind

Provides mental health group programming for teens and young adults with anxiety. Participants learn and practice strategies for coping with stress and anxiety. There is a focus on providing mindfulness techniques and educational workshops. Youth also receive free YMCA gym membership during these programs.

No Fee.

No Referral Required.

Bullying, Self-Harm and Suicide Prevention

NEED2 Suicide Prevention, Education and Support

2390 Arbutus Road, Victoria, BC, V8N 1V7

250-386-6328 or admin@need2.ca

www.need2.ca

Works to prevent suicide and reduce the stigma of talking about suicide. Provides community programs, training workshops, and emotional and crisis support. Programs include Suicide Education and Awareness and support groups for those who have experienced suicide loss.

No Fee.

No Referral Required.

Pacific Centre Family Services Association

#200-324 Goldstream Ave, Colwood, BC

250-478-3699 or pacificcentre@pcfsa.org

www.pacificcentrefamilyservices.org

Youth Services & YouthTalk

Provides email counselling, information and referrals by professional counsellors to support youth issues with bullying, depression, relationships, and anger management. Services are provided to youth aged 10-19. Services may include outreach, counselling, family mediation, crisis intervention and group activities.

No Fee.

Referrals can be made from community or family.

Mood Disorders (Depression, Postpartum Depression, Bipolar)

Island Health

1560 Church Avenue, Victoria, BC

1-888-494-3888

www.islandhealth.ca/

Integrated Mobile Crisis Response Team (IMCRT)

This response team provides nightly emergency service to support individuals experiencing crises throughout southern Vancouver Island. Services are provided to individuals and families experiencing immediate concerns related to mental health and addiction issues, including depression, dementia, psychosis, mania, suicide risk, and acute distress related to other psychiatric conditions.

No Fee.

Health Professional Referral Required.

Mental Health Recovery Partners – South Vancouver Island

941 Kings Road, Victoria, BC, V8T 1W7

250-384-4225 or admin@mhrp.ca

https://mhrp.ca/

Provides services for individuals and their families dealing with mental health challenges, including schizophrenia, psychosis, bipolar disorder, or major depression. Information, advocacy, and referrals may be provided. This organization runs support groups, including peer support and family counselling. One specialized program offers supports specifically to the families and friends of people with mental illness.

No Fee up to fixed maximum per family.

Adults must self-refer. No other referrals needed.

Stigma-Free Society

Fernwood Community Centre - 1240 Gladstone Avenue, Victoria, BC

250-381-1552

www.stigmafreezone.com

Women’s Peer Support Group

A group for adult women who are living with a mental health condition. Conditions may include bipolar disorder, schizophrenia, anxiety, and/or depression, but there is no requirement to have an official diagnosis. This group discusses several issues such as mental wellness, career building, self-care, and coping strategies. Resources are shared to support these issues.

No Fee.

No Referral Required.

Substance Abuse and Addiction

BC Responsible & Problem Gambling Program (RPGP)

PO Box 9311 Stn Prov Govt, Victoria, BC V8W 9N1

1-888-795-6111

www.bcresponsiblegambling.ca/

This program provides counselling and support to all BC residents who are dealing with gambling addiction. Services include a 24/7 Gambling Support Line, which can provide information about the program, additional supports, and referrals to registered counsellors.

No Fee.

No Referral Required.

Victoria Native Friendship Centre

Addiction Services Program

231 Regina Avenue, Victoria, BC, V8Z 1J6

250-384-3211

www.vnfc.ca

Addiction Services Programs & Youth Addiction Program

Provides services to First Nations individuals of all ages who are dealing with substance abuse. Supports include free counselling, and promotion of abstinence, prevention and harm reduction. The Centre may also provide referral to additional support groups and residential treatment facilities.

No Fee.

No Referral Required.

Dementia and Alzheimer's

Alzheimer’s Society of BC – Victoria Branch

202-306 Burnside Road West, Victoria, BC, V8Z 1M1

250-382-2052 or info.victoria@alzheimerbc.org

www.alzheimer.ca/en/bc

Provides information, education, and support services regarding Alzheimer’s disease and other dementias. Runs both in-person and tele-support groups for individuals and their families who are affected by dementia and Alzheimer’s.

No Fee.

No Referral Required.

Victoria Chinatown Care Society

555 Herald Street, Victoria, BC, V8W 1S5

250-381-4322 / 250-920-0318

www.victoriachinatowncare.ca

Residential Care Program & Adult Day Program

This organization provides information and services to individuals affected by physical and cognitive difficulties, including dementia and Alzheimer’s. Clients are supported through a personalized care plan, which includes consultation with nurses, occupational therapists, dietitians and other specialized therapists. Group programs are available to support socialization, well-being, and independence.

Adult Day Program: up to $10 a day.
Other programs may charge a small fee.

Medical Referral from Island Health Required.
